About me

I am a dedicated and passionate researcher in the field of botany. My academic career began with a scientific initiation at the University of São Paulo (USP), where I had the opportunity to deepen my studies on plant anatomy and hydraulics.

My research work during graduation resulted in an invitation to continue my studies at the master's level, where I further deepened my knowledge of ecology and the structures and functions of plants. In my master's thesis, the study of ecology, biomechanics and hydraulics of lianas was the central theme, which made me a specialist in the field.

After completing the master's degree, I decided to continue my academic career, this time dedicating myself to a doctorate (PhD). My research project aimed to investigate: (I) how the phylogenetic structure of a group of lianas is associated with environmental gradients, and (II) how the physiological/hydraulic mechanisms associated with the anatomical structure allow the survival of lianas in different tropical forests, taking into account factors such as water availability. My results have been and are being published in several international scientific journals.

Currently, I am an active researcher and committed to the advancement of knowledge in the field of botany, with experience in studies of plant ecology, plant anatomy, biomechanics and hydraulics of plants, especially lianas. Also, I hope that my research work can contribute to the society we live in, expanding our understanding of plants and showing their importance to our society.

I believe that by working together, we can make a difference, and I am dedicated to sharing my knowledge with others in order to help them achieve their own goals and make their own contributions to the field of botany.
